# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and inclusive environment for everyone participating in this project, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or other personal characteristics.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- communicating clearly, respectfully, and constructively,
- giving and accepting technical feedback in good faith,
- focusing criticism on ideas and changes rather than people,
- acknowledging mistakes and taking responsibility for their impact,
- respecting different viewpoints, experiences, and boundaries,
- prioritizing the health of the project and its community.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or threats,
- insulting, demeaning, or inflammatory comments,
- personal or political attacks unrelated to the project,
- unwelcome sexual attention or imagery,
- deliberate disruption of discussions or project work,
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission,
- repeated unwanted contact,
- retaliation against anyone who reports a concern,
- other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, commits, documentation, and other communication channels. It also applies when an individual officially represents the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

Report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior privately to the repository maintainers through GitHub. Do not disclose sensitive details in a public issue.

Reports should include, when possible:

- a description of the incident,
- links, screenshots, or other relevant context,
- when and where the incident occurred,
- any additional information that may help assess the report.

Maintainers will acknowledge and review reports promptly, protect the privacy and safety of everyone involved, and avoid conflicts of interest. A maintainer involved in an incident must not participate in handling that report.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may edit, remove, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Depending on the nature, severity, and history of a violation, maintainers may take one or more of the following actions:

1. **Correction** — a private or public request to correct inappropriate behavior.
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with clear consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction** — temporary suspension from project interactions.
4. **Permanent restriction** — permanent removal from project spaces.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and applied consistently. Good-faith reports will not result in retaliation, even when an investigation does not establish a violation.
